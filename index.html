Who Am I?

My name is Daniel Doyle. Stirred by strong iced coffee, hard workouts on the mats, and interesting engineering problems, I'm a software developer with a perchant for tackling challenging problems.

Always the kid that would take apart VCRs at home and try putting them together again to see how they ticked, the world of engineering captivated my imagination. Futurists like Nikolai Tesla, who dreamt of bettering the world around them using science and engineering, inspired me to pursue my degree and find a way to make a meaningful impact on the lives of those around me.

Originally a Mechnical Engineering student at the University of Illinois at Chicago, I found a passion for programming during my senior year when I took my first Computer Science course, CS 109: C++ Programming For Engineers. What I loved about programming was the hands-on nature of building software, seeing code that you actually write come to life was something I found very gratifying. That class inspired me to stay in school and change my major. A year later I graduated with a B.S. in Computer Science, concentration in Software Engineering.

My course work was focused on topics such as:

  • Object Oriented Programming
  • Relational Database Design & Implementation
  • Software Engineering & Design
  • Data Structures & Algorithms
  • Mobile Development
